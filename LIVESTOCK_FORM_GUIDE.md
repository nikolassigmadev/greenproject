# Livestock Product Form Guide

## Overview
When adding or editing meat, dairy, or egg products in the admin section, you'll now see a specialized form that captures detailed production information. This data directly impacts the sustainability score based on scientific research.

## How to Use

### 1. Select Animal Type
Choose the specific animal type:
- **Beef/Cow** - For beef products and dairy cows
- **Pork/Pig** - For pork products
- **Chicken** - For chicken meat and eggs
- **Turkey** - For turkey products
- **Lamb/Sheep** - For lamb and mutton
- **Salmon/Fish** - For fish and seafood
- **Other** - For other livestock

### 2. Housing/Production Method
Select how the animals are raised:

#### For Beef/Cattle:
- **Pasture-raised**: Animals roam freely on pasture (highest score)
- **Free-range**: Access to outdoor areas
- **Factory-farmed**: Industrial confinement systems (lowest score)
- **Intensive**: High-density operations

#### For Chicken/Eggs:
- **Free-range**: Outdoor access available
- **Pasture-raised**: Raised on pasture with vegetation
- **Caged**: Confined to cages (battery cages - lowest score)
- **Factory-farmed**: Indoor confinement systems

#### For Salmon/Fish:
- **Wild-caught**: Caught from natural habitats
- **Farm-raised**: Aquaculture operations

### 3. Feed Type
Choose the animals' diet:

#### For Beef/Cattle:
- **Grass-fed**: Natural grass diet (100% grass-fed - highest score)
- **Grain-fed**: High-grain diet for finishing
- **Mixed feed**: Combination of grass and grain
- **Organic feed**: USDA organic certified feed

#### For Chicken:
- **Organic feed**: USDA organic certified feed
- **Conventional feed**: Standard commercial feed
- **Vegetarian feed**: No animal byproducts
- **Non-GMO feed**: No genetically modified ingredients

#### For Salmon:
- **Wild diet**: Natural marine diet
- **Fish feed**: Commercial aquaculture feed
- **Organic feed**: Certified organic fish feed

### 4. Antibiotics Use
Select the antibiotics policy:
- **No Antibiotics**: Antibiotic-free production (highest score)
- **Therapeutic Only**: Only when medically necessary
- **Routine Use**: Regular preventive use
- **Growth Promoters**: Used for growth enhancement (lowest score)

### 5. Hormones Use
Choose hormone usage:
- **No Hormones**: No hormone supplementation (highest score)
- **Natural Hormones**: Natural hormone alternatives
- **Synthetic Hormones**: Synthetic growth hormones (lowest score)

### 6. Space Per Animal
(Not applicable for fish)
- **High**: Ample space, low stress (highest score)
- **Adequate**: Minimum welfare standards
- **Crowded**: Below welfare standards
- **Overcrowded**: Severely restricted (lowest score)

### 7. Slaughter Method
(Not applicable for fish)
- **Humane Certified**: Certified humane practices (highest score)
- **Standard**: Conventional methods
- **Unknown**: Method not specified

### 8. Certifications
Select all applicable certifications (each adds points to the score):

#### High-Value Certifications (+8-10 points):
- **Animal Welfare Approved**: Highest welfare standards
- **Regenerative Organic Certified**: Regenerative practices
- **Demeter Biodynamic**: Biodynamic farming
- **Global Animal Partnership (GAP) 5+**: Highest welfare level
- **Certified Humane**: Humane treatment standards
- **MSC Certified**: Sustainable seafood

#### Medium-Value Certifications (+5-7 points):
- **USDA Organic**: USDA organic standards
- **Grass-fed Certified**: Verified grass-fed
- **Pasture for Life**: 100% pasture-raised
- **Pasture-Raised**: Pasture access
- **Sustainable Seafood**: Basic sustainability

#### Basic Certifications (+3-4 points):
- **Non-GMO**: No genetically modified organisms
- **Free-Range Certified**: Verified free-range
- **No Antibiotics**: Antibiotic-free claim
- **Hormone-Free**: No hormone claim
- **Carbon Neutral**: Carbon offset programs

## Score Impact

The factors are weighted as follows:
- **Housing & Feed**: 27% of total score
- **Antibiotics & Hormones**: 18% of total score  
- **Space & Slaughter**: 15% of total score
- **Certifications**: 10% of total score
- **Environmental factors**: 30% (calculated automatically)

## Example Scenarios

### Excellent Product (90+ points):
- Animal: Beef
- Housing: Pasture-raised
- Feed: Grass-fed
- Antibiotics: No antibiotics
- Hormones: No hormones
- Space: High
- Certifications: Animal Welfare Approved, USDA Organic, Grass-fed Certified

### Poor Product (under 30 points):
- Animal: Chicken
- Housing: Caged
- Feed: Conventional feed
- Antibiotics: Growth promoters
- Hormones: Synthetic hormones
- Space: Overcrowded
- Certifications: None

## Tips for High Scores

1. **Choose pasture-raised or free-range** whenever possible
2. **Select grass-fed for ruminants** (beef, lamb, dairy)
3. **Opt for no antibiotics and no hormones**
4. **Ensure adequate space** for animal welfare
5. **Get credible certifications** (Animal Welfare Approved, Certified Humane)
6. **Consider wild-caught for fish** over farm-raised

The form automatically translates your selections into the product materials and calculates the appropriate sustainability score based on comprehensive animal welfare and environmental factors.
